Monthly Compliance Tasks

Every Month (2-3 hours)

Review access logs for anomalies

Check for unusual login patterns, failed authentications, or suspicious activity

Time: 30 min | Tools: CloudWatch, Datadog, SIEM

Verify backups completed successfully

Check database backups, config backups, and data replication status

Time: 15 min | Tools: AWS Backup, automated reports

Review security alerts and incidents

Investigate any security alerts, document incidents, track remediation

Time: 30 min | Tools: PagerDuty, Slack alerts, incident tracker

Check vulnerability scan results

Review latest scans, verify critical vulnerabilities are patched

Time: 30 min | Tools: Tenable, Qualys, Snyk

Monitor compliance dashboard

Check your compliance platform for any failing controls or missing evidence

Time: 15 min | Tools: Vanta, Secureframe, custom dashboard

Quarterly Tasks (4-6 hours)

Conduct access reviews

Review all user access across systems, revoke unnecessary permissions

Time: 2-3 hours | Who: Security lead + managers

Run vulnerability scans

External and internal vulnerability assessments of infrastructure

Time: 1 hour (review results) | Who: Engineering lead

Review and update policies

Check if security policies need updates based on business changes

Time: 1 hour | Who: Compliance owner

Test backup restoration

Actually restore from backup to verify recovery process works

Time: 1-2 hours | Who: DevOps/Infrastructure team

Vendor security reviews

Check for updated SOC2 reports from critical vendors

Time: 1 hour | Who: Procurement/Compliance

Annual Tasks (8-12 hours)

Security awareness training

All employees complete annual security training

Time: 1 hour per employee | Tools: KnowBe4, Securiti

Risk assessment

Comprehensive review of business and technology risks

Time: 4-6 hours | Who: Leadership + compliance team

Penetration testing

External pen test of production environment

Time: 2-3 hours (internal time) | Cost: $5-15k

Incident response tabletop exercise

Simulate a security incident to test your response plan

Time: 2-3 hours | Who: Entire leadership team

SOC2 renewal audit

Annual audit to renew your SOC2 certification

Time: Variable | Cost: $20-30k

Build a Compliance Calendar

Use this calendar template to stay on track (Google Calendar, Notion, or your project management tool):

1st of month: Review previous month's security logs and incidents
15th of month: Check backup success rates and vulnerability scans
End of Q1/Q3: Access reviews, policy updates, backup restoration tests
End of Q2/Q4: Same as Q1/Q3 + vendor reviews
Q4 (Oct/Nov): Annual tasks: Training, risk assessment, pen test, tabletop
Q1 (Jan/Feb): SOC2 renewal audit preparation and fieldwork

Signs Your Compliance Program is Slipping

üö® Red Flag: You can't remember the last access review

If it's been more than 3 months, you're overdue. Former employees might still have access.

üö® Red Flag: No one is checking logs or alerts

Security monitoring only works if someone actually reviews the alerts. Silence doesn't mean safety.

üö® Red Flag: Policies haven't been updated in a year+

Your business evolved. Your tech stack changed. Your policies should reflect current reality.

üö® Red Flag: You're not collecting evidence continuously

If you wait until audit time to gather evidence, you'll be scrambling and likely missing critical documentation.

üö® Red Flag: No one owns compliance

If compliance is "everyone's job," it's actually no one's job. Assign clear ownership.
